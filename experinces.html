Silver Star Computers/Technolgies

   So this one. Before i got this job, i was not working. I was learning an missing around with programing languages on my own. I had a great dad, who supported me during that time. Eventually life hit me and i realized i was not getting any money doing this and i need to make a change. My dad got into a partnership and was part owner of a computer shop in Washougal called Silver Star computers and he offered to let me work there. Now this was quite a change. My education and experience was in programming, not in computer hardware or repair. I had not even built my first computer by then. I was and still am a shy, introvert, with anxiety(both normal and social) to the point that my brain would just shut down when dealing with people. And here i was no idea what to do thrown into a job where i am expected to deal with people. It was not easy job and i stubbled the whole time. In my time there handled a lot of what you expect a person in a computer shop to do. I checked in and checked out customer computers. I handled the sales of items. I answered the phone. I did some of the repair work, learning what i needed along the way. I did virus removal, blue screen/shut down troubleshooting, computer clean ups, replaced/upgraded components, installed OS, data recovery, etc. I got experience creating and updating work tickets, don't remember what the application was. I got to use a program called parted magic a lot, we used this to check drives, recover data and clone. For data recovery i later moved on to using Minitool Partition Wizard wizard as it was easier to use. WE used quite a few anti-virus to clean up computers including a free open source one called Clamwin. Eventually i got to the point where i was also opening and closing the store when needed. Sadly my dad passed away suddenly. As a result the company sold to another gentlemen who kept the business but rebranded it as Silver Star Technologies. He kept me on and under him i did the same stuff as before. Sadly a combination of things lead to bad business and the owner closed the shop down. To me this time i would say is a bit of a mess. I learned a lot, i am able to handle the customer interactions a bit easier. But it was a stressful time having all the anxiety flowing through me all day. Just not a people person, and i sill not. Then losing my dad at the same time. Yea.
